General FAQ's about
Immigration to Canada

  • What are the eligibility requirements for immigrating to Canada?
    The eligibility requirements for immigrating to Canada depend on the immigration program you are applying for. For example, if you are applying for the Federal Skilled Worker Program, you must have at least one year of full-time work experience in a skilled occupation. Additionally, you must meet language proficiency requirements and score a minimum of 67 points on the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS). Other programs may have different eligibility requirements, so it is important to check the program's specific requirements before applying.
  • Can I bring my family with me when I immigrate to Canada?
    Yes, it is possible to sponsor your spouse, common-law partner, or dependent children to come with you to Canada. If your application is approved, your family members will be granted permanent residency status as well.
  • How long does it take to process an immigration application?
    The processing time for an immigration application can vary depending on the program and the volume of applications received by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC). Some applications can be processed in as little as six months, while others can take several years. You can check the current processing times on the IRCC website.
  • Do I need a job offer to immigrate to Canada?
    In some cases, having a job offer from a Canadian employer can be beneficial for your immigration application. However, it is not always necessary. Some immigration programs, such as the Express Entry system, do not require a job offer.
  • Can I work or study in Canada while waiting for my immigration application to be processed?
    If you are waiting for your permanent residency application to be processed, you may be eligible to apply for a work or study permit. However, eligibility requirements and application procedures for these permits are different and depend on the program you are applying for.
  • What is the difference between permanent residency and citizenship?
    Permanent residency is a status that allows you to live and work in Canada indefinitely. However, you are still considered a citizen of your home country. Citizenship is a process by which you become a citizen of Canada and can enjoy additional rights and privileges, such as voting and holding a Canadian passport.
  • How do I apply for immigration to Canada?
    The process for applying for immigration to Canada can be complex and requires various forms, documents, and fees. To start your application, you should first carefully review the specific eligibility requirements for the program you are applying for. Once you are eligible, you will need to fill out and submit the appropriate application form along with supporting documents and fees. It is recommended that you seek the help of a qualified immigration consultant or lawyer to assist with your application.
