Student

A study permit is most probably required to study in Canada if your program of study is more than 6 months and you are not exempted from this requirement due to any other applicable situations to your personal profile.

At KB Immigration Inc., we also provide the following services for students :

Study Permit

Canada is one of the top destinations in the world for international students seeking a high-quality education and a rich cultural experience. If you are considering studying in Canada, you will likely need a Study Permit.

A Study Permit is a document issued by the Canadian government that allows foreign nationals to study at designated learning institutions (DLIs) in Canada. DLIs include universities, colleges, and other institutions that are authorized to admit international students.

To apply for a Study Permit, you must have an offer of admission from a DLI in Canada. You will also need to provide proof of financial support to cover your tuition fees, living expenses, and any other costs associated with your studies in Canada.

In addition, you may need to provide evidence of English or French language proficiency, depending on the language of instruction at your chosen DLI. You may also need to complete a medical exam to show that you are in good health.

Once you have gathered all the necessary documents and information, you can apply for a Study Permit online or by paper. The application process can take several weeks or even months, so it's important to apply as early as possible.

If your application is approved, you will receive a Letter of Introduction from the Canadian government. You will need to present this letter, along with your passport and other documents, to a border officer when you arrive in Canada. The border officer will then issue your Study Permit.

With a Study Permit, you can study in Canada for the duration of your program of study. You may also be eligible to work part-time on campus or off-campus, depending on the terms of your permit.

If you are considering studying in Canada, a Study Permit is an essential requirement. For more information on applying for a Study Permit, contact us.

Study Permit/ Extensions and related Temporary Resident Visa Renewals from inside Canada

After students have obtained their study permits, we help students to get their study permits extensions if they want to continue their study when their initial study permit is about to expire. Once the study permit extension is approved, we would assist the students in submission of their temporary resident visa renewal. This is a necessary component of the process, to maintain their legal immigration status in Canada, while they complete their studies.

There are many other situations which might make you eligible for applying study permits from within Canada. These include sponsored family members, whose application for PR has been submitted and are in Canada, family members of those who already hold study of work permit and those who already hold study or work permit themselves, family or staff member of foreign representatives etc.

Post Graduate Work Permit

Are you an international student looking to stay and work in Canada after completing your studies? The Post Graduate Work Permit (PGWP) may be the solution!

The PGWP allows international graduates to work in Canada for up to three years after completing their studies. Not only does this provide valuable work experience, but it also allows graduates to gain Canadian work experience that can count towards permanent residency applications.

To be eligible for a PGWP, you must have completed a full-time program of at least eight months in duration at a Designated Learning Institution (DLI). You must also apply for the PGWP within 180 days of receiving your final transcripts.

The duration of your PGWP will depend on the length of your study program. Programs of less than eight months are not eligible for a PGWP, while programs longer than two years may be eligible for a three-year permit. Programs between eight months and two years in duration may be eligible for a permit whose duration matches the length of the program.

Applying for the PGWP involves submitting an application form, supporting documents (including your final transcript), and paying a fee. Processing times vary depending on the season, but in general it is advisable to apply as soon as possible after receiving your final transcripts.

The PGWP is a great opportunity for international graduates to gain valuable Canadian work experience and make a contribution to the Canadian economy. If you are an international graduate looking to stay and work in Canada, consider the Post Graduate Work Permit as a pathway to your future in Canada!

Changing the Institution or course

Are you an international student studying at a Canadian college but considering a change in your college? If so, we have some important information to help guide your decision-making process.

Firstly, it is important to understand that changing your college after arriving in Canada as a student of another Canadian college is possible, but it is not a simple process. There are several steps and conditions that must be met before making the change.

To begin with, you will need to obtain a letter of acceptance from the new college where you wish to transfer to. This letter should confirm that you have met all the admission requirements for the new program and that you have been accepted into the program.

Once you have your letter of acceptance, you will need to apply for a new study permit. This is a crucial step, as your current study permit is only valid for the institution and program you were accepted to. In order to be able to study at the new college, you must have a valid study permit that reflects the change in institution and program.

When applying for a new study permit, you will need to provide documentation from both your current and new institutions, demonstrating that you have met the necessary admission requirements and that you have been accepted into the new program. You will also need to provide evidence of sufficient funds to support yourself while studying at the new college.

It is important to note that changing your college mid-program may have an impact on your study permit and immigration status in Canada. You will need to ensure that you comply with all immigration regulations and requirements, and that you do not violate any conditions of your study permit, such as working off-campus eligibility and maintaining sufficient academic standing.

Overall, changing your college as an international student can be a complex process that requires careful planning and preparation. It is always advisable to seek out the guidance and support of our experienced immigration advisor to ensure that you understand all the steps involved and that you are able to make the best possible choices for your academic and immigration goals..

Open Work Permit for your Spouse

Are you planning to migrate to Canada with your spouse and wondering if they can work while you settle in? You'll be happy to hear that Canada offers an Open Work Permit for Spouses of international students and skilled workers. This visa allows your spouse to work for any employer in Canada without the need for a job offer before arrival, and it's not limited to a specific job or location.

To be eligible for an Open Work Permit for Spouses, your partner needs to be in Canada as an international student or skilled worker on a valid study or work permit. They must not be inadmissible to Canada and should demonstrate that they will support themselves during their stay. The application process differs depending on whether you're submitting an inland or outland application, so it's important to check the requirements before applying.

This work permit is valid for a maximum of two years, or the same duration as your partner's permit, whichever is shorter. It's a great opportunity for your spouse to gain Canadian work experience and contribute to your family's income while you settle in Canada.

With the right preparations and documentation, your spouse can soon be on their way to a fulfilling career in Canada!

Parent’s Visitor Visa

If you are a student in Canada and you want to invite your parents to come and visit you, you may need to apply for a parents visitor visa for Canada. This type of visa allows your parents to visit you in Canada for up to six months.

To apply for a parents visitor visa, your parents will need to provide certain documents and meet certain requirements. These can include proof of financial support, proof of ties to their home country, a valid passport, and a letter of invitation from you.

To prepare the letter of invitation, you will need to provide your full name, address, and contact information, as well as the purpose of your parents’ visit and how long they plan to stay in Canada. It’s important to be clear and concise in your letter of invitation.

Once the application is submitted, it can take a few weeks or even months for it to be processed. It’s important to plan ahead and apply for the visa well in advance of your parents’ planned visit.

If your parents are visiting Canada to attend your graduation ceremony or to help you move into a new apartment, it’s important to provide proof of these events and how they relate to your parents’ visit.

Overall, a parents visitor visa for Canada can be a great way to help your parents visit you while you are studying in Canada. Just be sure to follow all the necessary steps and requirements in the application process.
